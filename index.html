Donation Itemizer — Free Charitable Donation Tax Deduction Calculator

Donation Itemizer is a free browser-based tool that helps you track non-cash charitable donations and estimate their fair market value (FMV) for tax deduction purposes. Search hundreds of items, get value estimates from the Salvation Army and Goodwill valuation guides, and export a formatted PDF summary for your tax preparer.

How It Works

  1. Add a donation date — Record each trip to Goodwill, Salvation Army, or any 501(c)(3) organization with the date and organization name.
  2. Search for donated items — Browse or search hundreds of items across 7 categories: clothing, furniture, electronics, sporting goods, household items, books and media, and musical instruments. Each item shows a fair market value range based on condition.
  3. Set condition and quantity — Toggle between Poor, Good, and Excellent condition. Adjust the FMV within the published range using a slider. Set quantities for multiple identical items.
  4. Review your deduction total — See a live-updating running total with breakdowns by category and donation date. IRS threshold flags alert you when you need Form 8283 or a qualified appraisal.
  5. Export a PDF summary — Download a formatted report with all donation events, itemized values, and Form 8283 field references, ready to hand to your CPA or attach to your tax return.

IRS Compliance Thresholds

Donation Itemizer automatically flags when your donations cross key IRS thresholds:

  • Over $250 per donation event — Written acknowledgment from the receiving organization is required (per-event, not aggregate).
  • Over $500 total non-cash donations — Form 8283 Section A must be attached to your tax return.
  • Over $5,000 for a single item — A qualified appraisal is required and must be attached to Form 8283 Section B.
  • Items in poor condition — Generally not deductible under IRC Section 170(f)(16) unless valued over $500 with a qualified appraisal.

Fair Market Value Sources

FMV estimates in Donation Itemizer are sourced from the Salvation Army Valuation Guide and Goodwill Industries donation value guides. These are published charity valuation references — they are not IRS-determined or IRS-endorsed values. The IRS defines fair market value in Publication 561 as "the price that property would sell for on the open market." Always consult a qualified tax professional to verify values before claiming deductions on your tax return.

Item Categories

Track donations across these categories with condition-specific FMV ranges:

  • Clothing — Shirts, pants, dresses, outerwear, shoes, accessories, suits, sweaters, and more
  • Sporting Goods — Skis, bicycles, golf clubs, tennis rackets, exercise equipment, camping gear
  • Furniture — Sofas, tables, chairs, bed frames, dressers, desks, bookshelves, lamps
  • Electronics — Smartphones, laptops, monitors, tablets, TVs, gaming consoles (higher IRS scrutiny — depreciated value applies)
  • Household — Kitchenware, linens, towels, rugs, picture frames, decorative items
  • Books & Media — Hardcover and paperback books, DVDs, board games, toys
  • Instruments — Guitars, keyboards, and other musical instruments

Privacy & Data

Donation Itemizer runs 100% in your web browser. No data is ever sent to a server. Your donation information is stored locally on your device using browser localStorage. No account, no sign-up, no tracking. You can export a JSON backup of your data at any time and restore it on any device.
